History

Kelvin Nganeme Ejiogu as the first Worthy President. Col. Ejiogu, the founder, came from the Sacred Heart Commandery 366 Monrovia, Liberia to organize the Order in Nigeria with the approval of late Rt. Dr. G.M.P Okoye, the then Bishop of Enugu Catholic Diocese. Included among the founding fathers were;

Mission

The object of the said corporation shall be to create and foster a feeling of fraternity and fellowship among the various commanderies within this union, to improve their moral, mental and social condition and to aid, assist and support members of their families in case of want, sickness or death and to promote the welfare of the Roman Catholic religion.

To this end our Agenda shall be:

  • To foster a feeling of fraternity and fellowship among brothers;
  • to encourage the practice of civic virtues;
  • to Develop better conception of public duty;
  • To infuse into human society a broader loftier morality.

Charism Of Knighthood

Charism, according to Webster's Seventh New Collegiate Dictionary is an extraordinary power given to a Christian by the Holy Spirit for the good of the Church. It is also a personal magic or charm of leadership arousing special popular loyal or enthusiasm for a statement or military commander. The Catholic Church is a universal church. Her organization, structure and philosophy must be such that have a universal appeal and accommodate diverse interests and aspirations. Thus, in the domain of priesthood, we have many congregations, each with her own Charism that sets her apart from the others. In the domain of sisterhood or brotherhood, each membership and recognition from the general public. The only common denominator existing among the priests, brothers and sisters is the fact that they are all consecrated servants of God. In the domain of the laity, the Vatican Council has liberalized participation of the laity in the various activities and functions of the Church. With this liberalization, many organizations, associations and fraternal groupings have carved out for themselves their individual Charisms that propel them to group action, all aimed at advancing the course and the interest of the Mother Church. Apart from the consecrated servants of God, the Knights appear to play a leading role in the evangelization and lay apostolate of the Church. Their Charism includes being the defenders of the Church and waging war against injustice, corruption, defenders of the weak, democratic principles and freedom of worship according to one's conscience.

Where Does The Knight Derive His Strength?

Faith in Jesus Christ prayer, fasting and doing good to all manner of people without regard to creed, race or colour and full understanding and strict obedience to ones charism and the armour of that provides strength to the Knights. Yearly, monthly, weekly and daily meditations, reflections and prayers are further avenues for Knights to communicate with God on regular basis for guidance and direction. The Knights must cultivate the habit of living a meditative, reflective and prayerful monthly, weekly and daily life.
